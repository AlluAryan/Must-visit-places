Must-Visit Places in Agartala, Tripura

Discover the beauty and history of Agartala!

Ujjayanta Palace
A stunning royal palace built in 1901, known for its beautiful architecture and lush gardens. Now a state museum, it showcases the history and culture of Tripura.
Neermahal Palace
Located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake, Neermahal is the only water palace in Northeast India, featuring a blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture.
Tripura Sundari Temple
One of the 51 Shakti Peethas, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari and is a revered pilgrimage site, located in Udaipur near Agartala.
Jagannath Temple
Situated inside the Ujjayanta Palace grounds, this temple is known for its beautiful architecture and spiritual ambiance.
Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary
A haven for nature lovers, this sanctuary is home to clouded leopards, birds, lakes, and botanical gardens. Boating facilities are also available.
Heritage Park
A peaceful park in central Agartala showcasing Tripura's rich cultural and natural heritage through models, sculptures, and lush greenery.
Unakoti
A significant ancient pilgrimage site located near Kailashahar, Unakoti is renowned for its massive rock-cut sculptures of Hindu deities like Kal Bhairava, Lord Ganesha, and Lord Hanuman. Often described as a potential UNESCO World Heritage Site, it attracts thousands of devotees and history enthusiasts annually.
Jampui Hills
Located in North Tripura at an altitude of 1,000 meters, these hills are famous for orange plantations and the annual Orange Festival. The scenic beauty, tribal culture, and sunset views make it a favored destination for nature lovers.
Dumboor Lake
A natural wonder located near Amarpur Sub Division, known for its 48 islets and serene beauty. It offers a 45-minute boat ride to 'Narkelkunja,' also known as 'Coconut Lake'. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular spot for boating and picnicking. The best time to visit is during October to March when the weather is pleasant.
